# World & Setting

## Era

Medieval — roughly analogous to 11th–13th century Western Europe, but not historically bound. No gunpowder. No printing press. Literacy is rare outside monasteries and merchant families.

## Geography (to be developed)

- Malik's home: a small agricultural village, river-adjacent, near enough to a monastery town that traveling scholars pass through seasonally
- The wider world has universities (modeled on Bologna, Paris), apothecary guilds, a Church-adjacent institution that monitors magical practitioners
- Magic is known but rare — most people go a lifetime without meeting a practitioner

## The Science of This World

The intellectual framework for understanding life, death, and magic draws from:

**Galenic / Humoral medicine**
- The body is governed by four humors: blood, phlegm, yellow bile, black bile
- Health is balance; disease is imbalance; death is the final cooling and settling of humors
- Practitioners speak of the *vital humour* — a fifth, subtle fluid that carries animating force

**Aristotelian natural philosophy**
- Three layers of soul: *vegetative* (growth, nutrition — shared with plants), *sensitive* (sensation, movement — shared with animals), *rational* (thought — human only)
- Death proceeds in reverse order: rational soul departs first, then sensitive, then vegetative last
- A skilled enough necromancer is working in the window *before the vegetative soul fully disperses*

**Alchemical thinking**
- Matter tends toward corruption (entropy) unless sustained by *spiritus* — the animating breath woven through living tissue
- The necromancer reverses this tendency locally, at the cost of their own spiritus
- Extended use is dangerous not because magic is evil, but because you are spending yourself

**The System**
The LitRPG system exists in-world as a genuine metaphysical phenomenon — a kind of *universal ledger* that certain sensitive practitioners begin to perceive. Most never learn to read it clearly. Malik learns to read it fluently. Whether the system is God, a natural law, or something stranger is left open.

## Tone Notes

- The system boxes should feel like illuminated manuscript entries, not video game pop-ups
- Necromancy is not taboo here by default — it becomes controversial as Malik's power grows
- The Church's position: raising the dead delays the soul's journey. Heresy scale depends on duration and intent.
- Folk position: deeply superstitious, fearful, but also quietly hopeful (everyone has lost someone)
