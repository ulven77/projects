# Magic System

## Philosophy

Magic in this world is the manipulation of *spiritus* — the animating breath woven through all living matter. Every practitioner draws on their own spiritus reservoir (expressed as Mana in the system). Spending spiritus beyond your ceiling causes physical degradation. Practitioners who spend too much too fast age rapidly, go blind, lose sensation.

Necromancy is specifically the manipulation of *entropy direction* — forcing spiritus to flow backward through dying or dead tissue, restoring order from disorder. It is expensive because entropy is the natural state. You are fighting physics.

## Necromancy Mechanics

### The Entropic Window
Every organism has a window post-death during which restoration is possible:

| Organism | Window | Mana Cost (approx.) |
|----------|--------|---------------------|
| Insect | minutes | 10–20 |
| Small animal (rat, bird) | 1–2 hours | 80–200 |
| Large animal (dog, sheep) | 30 min | 300–600 |
| Human (fresh) | 15–45 min | 800–2000 |
| Human (cooled, hours) | minutes only | 3000+ |
| Human (days) | requires Binding first | 8000+ |

Windows shrink with ambient heat (decomposition accelerates spiritus dispersal).
Windows extend with cold (northern practitioners are more powerful by circumstance).

### The Cost Problem
Malik's starting ceiling: 14 Mana.
A child human: ~800 Mana.
This gap is the engine of the entire story. He spends his life expanding his ceiling — through training, sacrifice, and eventually, structural changes to his own body.

### The Lich Question
True lichdom in this world is not undeath. It is *self-anchoring* — the practitioner permanently binds their rational soul to their own body, preventing dispersal at natural death. The body continues to decay unless maintained with spiritus. The practitioner must continuously spend energy simply to persist.

The moral weight: a lich is not immortal. They are in perpetual debt. They must keep paying for every hour of existence. The question the story asks is: *what are you paying for?*

## Other Magic Types (brief)

**Healing (Vital Arts)** — common, Church-sanctioned, accelerates the body's own repair. Requires living tissue. Cannot reverse death.

**Geomancy** — manipulation of earth and stone. Guild-controlled. Used in construction, mining.

**Pyromancy** — rare, feared, no institutional home. Practitioners often executed.

**Compulsion (Mind Arts)** — illegal in most jurisdictions. Perceived as soul violation.

**Sight** — passive perception of spiritus fields. Common minor talent. Malik has this strongly — he sees death approaching before it arrives.
