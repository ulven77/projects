# Malik — Life Arc

## Core Character Truth
Malik is not driven by grief, ambition, or darkness. He is driven by *precision* — the need to understand exactly how things work, and love made structural: if he understands death completely, he can protect the people he loves from it. The tragedy is that understanding death completely requires him to become it.

---

## Stage 1 — The Rural Boy (ages 6–10)

**Setting:** Small agricultural village. Father is a miller or carpenter. Mother has some knowledge of herbs — not a practitioner, but observant.

**Character:** Gentle, curious, slow to anger. Cries easily. Picks up worms off the road after rain. Catches and releases. Talks to animals.

**Power state:** Latent. No system awareness. Just warmth in his hands when something is hurt near him.

**Inciting incident:** Kills the caterpillar accidentally. Restores it without understanding what he did. First system notification — he doesn't see it, but the reader does.

**Key themes:** Innocence. The instinct to restore. Not knowing the cost.

**Story beats:**
- Restores a mouse his cat brought in — drains fully, sick for a week, doesn't know why
- Village elder notices his hands go warm around the dying livestock, says nothing
- Plays with shadow puppets of animals, doesn't realize he's practicing Sight

---

## Stage 2 — The Necromant Infant (ages 10–13)

**Setting:** Still the village. A traveling monk-scholar passes through and notices the boy.

**Character:** Beginning to ask questions no one can answer. Frustrated by ignorance — his own and others'. First experience of being feared (a neighbor's dog died in his arms; the dog walked three steps before collapsing again; the neighbor saw).

**Power state:** Tier 0 operational. System becomes faintly visible — he thinks it's fever-dreams. Mana ceiling ~40 after two years of unconscious exercise.

**Key relationship:** The monk-scholar (Brother Aldric) — not a necromancer, but a natural philosopher who recognizes a practitioner when he sees one. Gives Malik his first framework: Aristotle's three souls. Lends him a book he cannot yet read.

**Key themes:** The gap between instinct and understanding. Being named before you name yourself.

**Story beats:**
- Restores a lamb; whole village sees; reactions split
- First conscious system notification — he is terrified
- Aldric explains: *"What you have is not evil. It is early."*
- Aldric leaves. Malik is alone again, but literate now.

---

## Stage 3 — The Necromant Child (ages 13–17)

**Setting:** Still village, but now he travels to the nearest monastery town seasonally to access texts.

**Character:** Methodical. Begins keeping a journal of every restoration — species, time since death, mana cost, outcome. A scientist before he has the word. Still emotionally open, but learning to be careful about showing it.

**Power state:** Tier 1 operational. Mana ceiling ~200. Beginning to perceive other practitioners' spiritus fields. Beginning to understand the Entropic Window.

**Key relationship:** A young apothecary's apprentice in the town (potential first companion — see companions.md). She doesn't have power but she has Galen, she has access to cadaver knowledge, and she asks better questions than he does.

**Key themes:** Science as love. The journal as intimacy. What it means to be useful vs. what it means to be right.

**Story beats:**
- First failed restoration on a human — child who drowned; window too long; Malik collapses trying
- First success on a human — a young man, fresh wound, Malik barely manages it, Mana ceiling cracks upward
- Church representative visits the town, asks questions. Malik learns his practice has a name and a legal status.
- Begins disguising restorations as healing

---

## Stage 4 — Scholar of Medicine (ages 17–25)

**Setting:** University city. Malik attends as a medical student — the closest legitimate institution to what he actually is.

**Character:** Disciplined. Increasingly aware that his power outpaces his understanding of *why* it works. Spends as much time in the library as in clinic. Develops a reputation as an unusually gifted healer who never loses patients.

**Power state:** Tier 2 operational. Mana ceiling ~1200. Now routinely restoring humans within the hour. Begins experimenting with Binding (Tier 0) — talking to the recently dead to understand what they experienced.

**Key relationship:** A professor of natural philosophy (master Ezra or similar) who suspects what Malik is, and instead of reporting him, becomes his thesis advisor. The academic partnership of a lifetime.

**Key themes:** Legitimacy vs. truth. What institutions protect and what they suppress. The ethics of interviewing the dead.

**Story beats:**
- Restores a colleague who died of fever — covers it as "he rallied" — but the colleague *knows* something happened
- First Binding conversation with a recently dead patient — learns death is not a wall, it's a corridor
- Writes his thesis on *The Persistence of Vital Calor in Post-Mortem Tissue* — technically accurate, practically incendiary
- Church auditor reads the thesis. Malik is called to account. Master Ezra protects him.
- Graduates. Has to leave the city.

---

## Stage 5 — The Aware Necromancer (ages 25–35)

**Setting:** Itinerant. Moves through towns as a physician-for-hire. Develops a small practice. Acquires companions.

**Character:** Fully self-aware about what he is. No longer hiding from himself, though still careful in public. The warmth that drove him as a child has become a precise instrument. He is happy, mostly. He has people he loves.

**Power state:** Tier 2–3. Mana ceiling ~3000. Begins pushing into Soul Tethering — keeping people days past death. This is where the Church has a real problem with him.

**Key themes:** Living with a secret. The difference between hiding and being private. What love demands.

**Story beats:**
- Saves a village from plague — works openly for the first time; his reputation goes regional
- Loses someone he couldn't save — the first real limit that matters
- Begins researching the ceiling problem systematically: can mana be expanded beyond natural limits?
- An Inquisitor begins tracking him

---

## Stage 6 — Priest / Healer (ages 35–50)

**Setting:** A specific town where he is known and trusted. A de facto community healer with an unusual theology.

**Character:** Integrated. He is Malik the physician and Malik the necromancer and Malik the natural philosopher, and he has stopped treating these as separate people. The community around him knows, in the vague way that communities know things they don't want to say clearly.

**Power state:** Tier 3–4. Mana ceiling ~8000. He has learned to draw on external spiritus sources — dying forests, battlefields, places of concentrated death. This is efficient but spiritually heavy.

**Key themes:** Community vs. institution. The ethics of anchoring souls. What the dead owe the living (nothing) and what the living owe the dead (a proper farewell).

**Story beats:**
- Establishes a practice of *consultation* — speaking to the recently dead on behalf of families, for grief closure. Becomes beloved.
- Anchors a dying companion's soul temporarily — the companion's choice, fully informed, as an experiment in willing death-deferral
- The Inquisitor arrives. Community hides him, but barely.
- He performs a high-profile restoration that cannot be hidden — a lord's son, days dead. He saves the boy but must flee.
- Loses the town. Loses some companions. Gains clarity.

---

## Stage 7 — Naturalist Necromancer (ages 50–65)

**Setting:** Wilderness and ruins. Malik retreats from human society to study death without interference. He has one or two companions left.

**Character:** The gentleness is still there, but it lives in a different house now. Less social warmth, more structural love — he works to understand death so completely that no one he cares about ever falls to ignorance again. He is occasionally frightening without meaning to be.

**Power state:** Tier 4. Mana ceiling ~15000. Experimenting with Permanent Anchor on other organisms. Maintains a small menagerie of long-dead creatures in various states of anchored life. Not cruelty — science. They are fed, comfortable, not in pain. But still.

**Key themes:** The naturalist's detachment as a form of love. When does understanding become control? The last companions.

**Story beats:**
- Produces the definitive written work on necromantic theory — the book that will change his world
- The book is burned by the Church in every city it reaches
- He copies it by hand. Sends it to his last students.
- Begins to understand the lich question personally — not as a plan, but as a logical endpoint

---

## Stage 8 — The Lich (ages 65+)

**Setting:** Isolated, but not lonely. He is the experiment now.

**Character:** Recognizably Malik. The boy who wept over a caterpillar is still in there — you see him in how he handles dying animals, how he talks to the last companion, how he apologizes to his own body for what he's doing to it.

**Power state:** Tier 5. Self-anchored. Spiritus becomes structural rather than expendable — he is perpetually paying to exist, and he finds the arithmetic clarifying. Every action must be worth the cost of being alive to take it.

**Key themes:** What is the cost of permanence? Not immortality — *persistence*. The lich doesn't live forever. He pays forever. What is he buying with that payment? The answer is the end of the book.

**Final image echo:** A caterpillar on a leaf. He sits with it. He does not restore it. He watches it die, and he counts every stage, and he writes it all down.
